World Cup 2026 | Match schedule, fixtures & stadiums

The 23rd FIFA Men's World Cup runs from June 11 to July 19, 2026 across the United States, Canada, and Mexico — the first three-nation co-hosted tournament in World Cup history. The format expands to 48 teams and a record 104 matches played in 16 host cities. Here's everything you need to know about the schedule, fixtures, and stadiums, plus where to watch every match live on Yalla Live.

Tournament overview

For the first time since the World Cup was expanded from 24 to 32 teams in 1998, the format changes again — this time growing to 48 teams divided into 12 groups of four. After the group stage, the top two from each group plus the eight best third-placed teams advance to a new Round of 32, which feeds the standard knockout bracket (Round of 16, quarter-finals, semi-finals, final). It's the biggest World Cup ever staged, with matches scheduled across three time zones and 16 venues spanning more than 4,000 miles.

Quick facts

  • Host nations: United States (11 venues), Canada (2 venues), Mexico (3 venues)
  • Dates: June 11 – July 19, 2026
  • Teams: 48
  • Group stage: 12 groups of 4 teams
  • Knockouts: Round of 32 → Round of 16 → Quarter-finals → Semi-finals → Final
  • Total matches: 104
  • Opening match: Estadio Azteca, Mexico City — June 11, 2026
  • Final: MetLife Stadium, East Rutherford, New Jersey — July 19, 2026
  • Defending champions: Argentina (won Qatar 2022)

Match schedule by phase

The tournament is split into a group stage and four knockout rounds. Below is the phase-by-phase breakdown of the 2026 World Cup calendar.

Group stage — June 11 to 27, 2026

All 48 teams play three group-stage matches. Each of the 12 groups sees six fixtures spread across the first 17 days of the tournament. The top two finishers from every group advance automatically; the eight best third-placed sides also progress to the Round of 32. Expect three to four matches per day, with the US, Canadian, and Mexican kick-off windows staggered to maximise global TV audiences.

Round of 32 — June 28 to July 3, 2026

A new knockout round, introduced specifically for the expanded 48-team format. The 32 surviving sides play 16 single-leg elimination matches over six days. Extra time and penalties decide ties; no replays.

Round of 16 — July 4 to 7, 2026

Eight last-16 ties played across the United States, Canada, and Mexico, with one extra rest day before the quarter-finals.

Quarter-finals — July 9 to 11, 2026

Four quarter-final matches over three days, all hosted in major US stadiums. By this point the field narrows to the eight best teams in the world.

Semi-finals — July 14 and 15, 2026

Two semi-final matches on consecutive nights, scheduled in the largest American venues. Winners advance to the final; losers contest the third-place play-off.

Third-place play-off — July 18, 2026

The two beaten semi-finalists play one last match the day before the final to decide bronze.

Final — Sunday, July 19, 2026 · MetLife Stadium

The 23rd FIFA World Cup Final will be played at MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford, New Jersey, home of the NFL's New York Giants and Jets, with a capacity of roughly 82,500 for the tournament. It's the first World Cup Final held on US soil since the iconic 1994 decider at the Rose Bowl.

The 16 host cities and stadiums

Sixteen venues were confirmed in 2022 — eleven across the United States, three in Mexico, and two in Canada. The list below covers each host city, the tournament stadium, and what to expect.

United States (11)

  • Atlanta — Mercedes-Benz Stadium
  • Boston — Gillette Stadium (Foxborough, MA)
  • Dallas — AT&T Stadium (Arlington, TX)
  • Houston — NRG Stadium
  • Kansas City — GEHA Field at Arrowhead Stadium
  • Los Angeles — SoFi Stadium (Inglewood, CA)
  • Miami — Hard Rock Stadium (Miami Gardens)
  • New York / New Jersey — MetLife Stadium (final venue)
  • Philadelphia — Lincoln Financial Field
  • San Francisco Bay Area — Levi's Stadium (Santa Clara, CA)
  • Seattle — Lumen Field

Canada (2)

  • Toronto — BMO Field (expanded for 2026)
  • Vancouver — BC Place

Mexico (3)

  • Guadalajara — Estadio Akron
  • Mexico City — Estadio Azteca (opening match venue)
  • Monterrey — Estadio BBVA

Estadio Azteca in Mexico City makes history again as the first stadium to host matches at three separate World Cups (1970, 1986, and 2026). MetLife Stadium in New Jersey hosts the final and, with around 82,500 expected attendees, will be one of the highest-capacity finals in tournament history.

Qualified teams & confederation slots

The three host nations — USA, Canada, and Mexico — qualified automatically. The remaining 45 places are distributed across the six confederations as follows:

  • UEFA (Europe): 16 direct slots
  • CAF (Africa): 9 direct + 1 inter-confederation play-off
  • AFC (Asia): 8 direct + 1 inter-confederation play-off
  • CONMEBOL (South America): 6 direct + 1 inter-confederation play-off
  • CONCACAF (North/Central America & Caribbean): 3 hosts + 3 direct + 2 inter-confederation play-offs
  • OFC (Oceania): 1 direct + 1 inter-confederation play-off
  • Inter-confederation play-off tournament: 2 final slots decided March 2026
