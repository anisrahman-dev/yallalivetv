World Cup 2026 | Match schedule, fixtures & stadiums

June 11, 2026

Find out how to watch FIFA World Cup 2026 live streaming online, match schedule, fixtures, and stadium details. The FIFA Men's World Cup 2026 is the 23rd edition of the tournament, starting from 11 June to 19 July 2026. The league features 104 matches between 48 teams across 16 host cities in the United States, Canada, and Mexico. The format includes a group stage of 12 groups of four, followed by a Round of 32 and standard knockout rounds. Currently, Argentina is the defending champion.

2026 FIFA World Cup Overview

For the first time since the tournament expanded from 24 to 32 teams in 1998, the format grows again — this time to 48 teams divided into 12 groups of four. The top two from each group plus the eight best third-placed sides advance to a new Round of 32, feeding the standard knockout bracket (Round of 16 → quarter-finals → semi-finals → final). It's the biggest World Cup ever staged, with matches scheduled across three time zones and 16 venues spanning more than 4,000 miles. FIFA's 2026 edition runs alongside the UEFA Champions League knockout calendar, but no club football clashes with the group stage.

FIFA World Cup 2026 Live Streaming

Several global TV broadcasters and digital media giants have purchased live broadcast and streaming rights for the FIFA World Cup 2026. Football fans from all over the globe can find out where to watch every match on TV and online — most major markets have at least one free-to-air option for the headline games.

United States

FOX Sports holds English-language broadcast rights and Telemundo holds Spanish-language rights through the 2026 cycle. FOX will air matches across FOX, FS1, and FS2, with full streaming on the Fox Sports app and Fox One (for cable cord-cutters). Telemundo coverage extends to Universo and streaming via Peacock. Subscription is typically required for streaming-only access (Fubo TV ~US$85/month, Hulu+ Live TV ~$83/month, YouTube TV ~$83/month).

United Kingdom & Ireland

BBC and ITV share rights and broadcast every match free-to-air. Streaming is included with BBC iPlayer and ITVX at no cost — viewers just need a valid TV licence (UK) or an Irish region account. This is the cheapest way to watch the entire tournament anywhere in the world.

India & Subcontinent

Sony Sports Network holds TV rights and is expected to air the matches in English, Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, and Malayalam across Sony Sports Ten 1, Ten 2, and Ten 3. Streaming is via JioHotstar (the merged JioCinema + Disney+ Hotstar platform). Free, ad-supported streams are available alongside premium plans starting from ₹149/month for mobile and ₹499+/month for multi-device 4K.

Middle East & North Africa (MENA)

beIN Sports remains the official broadcaster across the MENA region, covering 24 countries including Saudi Arabia, UAE, Egypt, Qatar, and Morocco. All 104 matches are available in Arabic and English commentary. The beIN Sports CONNECT app handles streaming for subscribers, typically priced AED 75–125/month depending on country and bundle.

Canada

Bell Media holds rights for both English (TSN) and French (RDS) broadcasts. As host nation Canada will get full coverage of all 104 matches. Streaming is included with TSN+ subscriptions (CA$24.99/month) or via TSN add-ons on most major TV providers.

Australia

SBS retains free-to-air rights for a select number of matches including all of Australia's group-stage games, both semi-finals, and the final, streamed for free on SBS On Demand. Optus Sport carries every match exclusively (AU$24.99/month) with multi-language commentary.

Mexico

TelevisaUnivision and TV Azteca share Mexico's broadcast rights, both airing free-to-air over Canal 5, Las Estrellas, Azteca 7, and Azteca Uno. Streaming is via ViX (Televisa's platform) and Azteca Deportes app — both available free with ads or premium tiers from MXN$99/month.

Sub-Saharan Africa

SuperSport provides full TV and digital coverage via DStv across 48 African countries. The DStv Premium package (~R900/month in South Africa, varying by region) includes all SuperSport channels plus app streaming on DStv Stream.

Brazil & South America

Grupo Globo holds rights in Brazil (TV Globo, SporTV, and the ge / Globoplay streaming platforms). In Argentina, broadcasts air on TV Pública and TyC Sports. Most CONMEBOL countries get free-to-air coverage of national-team matches plus pay-TV options for the full bracket.

Asia-Pacific

Japan: NHK and ABEMA jointly cover all 104 matches free, with ABEMA streaming free in HD. South Korea: KBS, MBC, and SBS share the rights, broadcasting matches free-to-air. China: CCTV-5 retains exclusive broadcasting rights. Southeast Asia: Astro (Malaysia), Mediacorp (Singapore), and Vidio (Indonesia) handle local distribution.

FIFA World Cup 2026 Teams & Top Contenders

Forty-eight nations qualify for the 2026 World Cup — the three hosts (USA, Canada, Mexico) plus 45 places distributed across the six confederations: UEFA gets 16 direct slots, CAF 9, AFC 8, CONMEBOL 6, CONCACAF 3 (in addition to the hosts), OFC 1, and two final slots are decided at an inter-confederation play-off tournament. Below are the headline contenders going into the tournament.

1. Argentina (Defending Champions)

Captain: Lionel Messi (likely his fifth and final World Cup)
Coach: Lionel Scaloni
Key players: Lionel Messi, Lautaro Martínez, Julián Álvarez, Emiliano Martínez, Cristian Romero, Enzo Fernández, Alexis Mac Allister
Argentina won Qatar 2022 on penalties against France and arrive as one of the favourites again. Scaloni's pressing system and Messi-led attack remains the team to beat in CONMEBOL.

2. France

Captain: Kylian Mbappé
Coach: Didier Deschamps
Key players: Kylian Mbappé, Antoine Griezmann, Aurélien Tchouaméni, Eduardo Camavinga, Ousmane Dembélé, William Saliba
Beaten finalists in 2022, France remain the deepest squad in world football. Mbappé enters his prime, and Deschamps will be looking for a third title.

3. Brazil

Captain: Neymar / Casemiro
Coach: Carlo Ancelotti (first World Cup with Seleção)
Key players: Vinicius Júnior, Rodrygo, Casemiro, Marquinhos, Raphinha, Endrick
Seven-time finalists, five-time winners — Brazil chase a sixth title to extend their all-time lead. Ancelotti brings a structured European blueprint to Brazil's traditional flair.

4. Spain (Euro 2024 Champions)

Captain: Álvaro Morata
Coach: Luis de la Fuente
Key players: Lamine Yamal, Pedri, Rodri, Nico Williams, Dani Olmo, Aymeric Laporte
Reigning European champions and arguably the most exciting young squad in the tournament. Lamine Yamal will be one of the breakout stars of WC 2026.

5. England

Captain: Harry Kane
Coach: Thomas Tuchel
Key players: Harry Kane, Jude Bellingham, Bukayo Saka, Phil Foden, Declan Rice, Cole Palmer
Tuchel is England's first foreign manager since 2012. After back-to-back Euro final losses, England's "golden generation" wants its first major trophy since 1966.

6. Germany

Captain: Joshua Kimmich
Coach: Julian Nagelsmann
Key players: Florian Wirtz, Jamal Musiala, Joshua Kimmich, Kai Havertz, Antonio Rüdiger
Eliminated in the group stages of the last two World Cups — Nagelsmann's rebuild around Musiala and Wirtz aims to restore Germany's place at the top.

7. Portugal

Captain: Cristiano Ronaldo (likely final World Cup)
Coach: Roberto Martínez
Key players: Cristiano Ronaldo, Bruno Fernandes, Bernardo Silva, Rúben Dias, Vitinha, João Neves
Possibly Ronaldo's last World Cup — Portugal will be highly motivated to send their captain off with a title. A loaded midfield and elite defenders make them a genuine dark horse.

8. Netherlands

Captain: Virgil van Dijk
Coach: Ronald Koeman
Key players: Virgil van Dijk, Cody Gakpo, Frenkie de Jong, Memphis Depay, Xavi Simons
Quarter-finalists in Qatar 2022, semi-finalists at Euro 2024 — the Dutch are quietly consistent and dangerous on the day.

9. USA (Host Nation)

Captain: Christian Pulisic
Coach: Mauricio Pochettino
Key players: Christian Pulisic, Tyler Adams, Weston McKennie, Sergiño Dest, Tim Weah, Yunus Musah
As host nation, the USMNT carries enormous expectation. Pochettino's experienced coaching combined with the most talented American squad ever assembled targets a deep run.

10. Morocco

Captain: Romain Saïss / Hakim Ziyech
Coach: Walid Regragui
Key players: Achraf Hakimi, Hakim Ziyech, Sofyan Amrabat, Youssef En-Nesyri, Brahim Díaz
The first African and Arab nation to reach a World Cup semi-final (Qatar 2022). Morocco's defensive solidity and counter-attacking style make them a danger to anyone.

11. Mexico (Host Nation)

Captain: Edson Álvarez
Coach: Javier Aguirre
Key players: Edson Álvarez, Hirving Lozano, Raúl Jiménez, Santiago Giménez, Luis Chávez
The most-supported Latin American team in a World Cup will play at Estadio Azteca on home soil. Mexico will be aiming to break their "round-of-16 curse" — they've gone out at that stage in every World Cup from 1994 to 2018.

12. Canada (Host Nation)

Captain: Alphonso Davies
Coach: Jesse Marsch
Key players: Alphonso Davies, Jonathan David, Stephen Eustáquio, Tajon Buchanan, Cyle Larin
Their second consecutive World Cup after a 36-year absence ended in 2022. As co-hosts they get every benefit possible and aim for their first-ever group-stage win.

Other genuine contenders include Belgium, Croatia, Uruguay, Colombia, Senegal, Japan, and Italy (if they qualify through the play-offs). FAQs

Which channel will broadcast the FIFA World Cup 2026 in the United States? expand_more

FOX Sports holds English-language rights and Telemundo holds Spanish-language rights. Matches will air on FOX, FS1, FS2, Telemundo, and Universo, with streaming via the Fox Sports app and Peacock.

Where can I watch FIFA World Cup 2026 live online in India? expand_more

JioHotstar is the official streaming platform, with TV broadcast on the Sony Sports Network. Both free (ad-supported) and premium (₹149+/month) tiers are available on JioHotstar.

Is the FIFA World Cup 2026 free to watch? expand_more

Yes in several markets: BBC and ITV in the UK, SBS in Australia (selected matches), TV Azteca in Mexico, NHK and ABEMA in Japan, and most European public broadcasters cover the tournament free-to-air. Yalla Live also indexes free stream links for every fixture.

What time do FIFA World Cup 2026 matches start? expand_more

Kick-off times are staggered across three time zones. Typical windows are 12:00, 15:00, 18:00, and 21:00 ET. In Asia, the same matches play overnight or early morning (e.g., 21:00 ET = 06:30 IST next day; 03:00 AM in Pakistan; 09:00 in Tokyo).

When and where is the FIFA World Cup 2026 final? expand_more

The final is on Sunday, 19 July 2026 at MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford, New Jersey, with kick-off at 15:00 ET.

How many teams play in the FIFA World Cup 2026? expand_more

48 teams — up from 32 in Qatar 2022, the largest field in World Cup history. They play 104 matches in total, distributed across 16 host cities in three nations.

Can I watch the World Cup 2026 in Hindi or Arabic? expand_more

Yes. In India, Sony Sports Ten 3 carries Hindi commentary and JioHotstar offers Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, and Malayalam audio tracks. In MENA, beIN Sports provides full Arabic commentary across all 104 matches.

Who are the defending FIFA World Cup champions? expand_more

Argentina won the 2022 FIFA World Cup in Qatar, beating France on penalties in one of the greatest finals ever played. They head into 2026 as one of the favourites alongside France, Brazil, Spain, England, Germany, and Portugal.
