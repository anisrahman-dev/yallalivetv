How to Watch Football in MENA — beIN Sports & Free Alternatives

The Middle East and North Africa (MENA) region is one of the most passionate football markets on Earth — Premier League viewership is higher per capita in Egypt and Morocco than in much of Europe. This guide covers how to watch football in the 22 MENA countries: the dominant role of beIN Sports, free alternatives like TOD, SSC, and Shahid, plus what each country gets natively — including Saudi Arabia, UAE, Egypt, Morocco, Jordan, Qatar, Lebanon, and beyond.

beIN Sports — The Dominant Force

beIN Sports (originally Al Jazeera Sport, rebranded in 2014) holds rights to almost every premium football competition across MENA — Premier League, La Liga, Serie A, Bundesliga, Ligue 1, Champions League, Europa League, FIFA World Cup, AFC Asian Cup, and the local Saudi Pro League. The beIN Sports HD package carries 17+ HD channels with Arabic and English commentary on every competition.

beIN Sports subscription tiers

  • beIN Sports HD package: ~AED 75/month (UAE), QAR 100/month (Qatar). Includes all 17 channels.
  • beIN Premium 12-month package: Bundle with entertainment channels for ~AED 100-125/month.
  • beIN Sports CONNECT (streaming-only): ~AED 80-100/month, mobile + web. Works across all MENA countries.
  • Pay-per-day (Egypt, Morocco): Reduced-price daily passes during big tournaments like the World Cup.

Country-by-Country Football Coverage in MENA

🇸🇦 Saudi Arabia

SSC (Saudi Sports Channels) carries the Saudi Pro League free over-the-air, plus selected international matches. Shahid VIP (MBC's streaming platform) bundles SSC channels with entertainment for SAR 30/month. beIN Sports covers all the major European leagues — separately required.

🇦🇪 United Arab Emirates

eVision / Etisalat Switch TV bundles beIN Sports CONNECT into mobile/internet packages. TOD (beIN's standalone streaming service) is available for AED 35/month for the sports tier. Dubai Sports Channel covers local UAE football free-to-air.

🇪🇬 Egypt

The largest football fan base in MENA. ON Sport (formerly Time Sport) carries the Egyptian Premier League and African football (CAF) competitions free-to-air. beIN Sports required for European leagues. WatchIT is Egypt's MBC-owned streaming app, ~EGP 90/month.

🇲🇦 Morocco

Arryadia (TVM) is Morocco's free national sports channel, covering Botola Pro, AFCON, and Moroccan national team matches. beIN Sports is widely subscribed for European football. AFCON 2025 — hosted in Morocco — aired free across the country.

🇶🇦 Qatar

beIN Sports' home country. Coverage is the most comprehensive in the region. Alkass Sports Channels are free-to-air and cover Qatar Stars League plus local football. World Cup matches were free on Alkass during Qatar 2022.

🇰🇼 Kuwait & 🇧🇭 Bahrain

Both countries' national broadcasters (Kuwait TV Sport, Bahrain Sports) cover local Gulf football free-to-air. beIN Sports the dominant pay-TV option for European football. TOD streaming popular among younger fans.

🇯🇴 Jordan & 🇱🇧 Lebanon

Jordan Sports TV and Lebanon's Télé Liban cover national-team football free. beIN Sports widely used for European matches. Both countries have lower beIN penetration than the Gulf — many fans use IPTV or aggregator sites.

🇹🇳 Tunisia & 🇩🇿 Algeria & 🇱🇾 Libya

North African Maghreb countries have strong local sports broadcasters (Watanya 1 in Tunisia, EPTV in Algeria) carrying local league football and African competitions. beIN Sports for European football. Often beIN packages bundled with home internet via Topnet (Tunisia) or Algerie Telecom.

🇮🇶 Iraq & 🇸🇾 Syria

Free-to-air national channels (Al Iraqiya Sports, Syrian Sports) cover national team and local football. beIN Sports availability varies by region. Many fans rely on IPTV and aggregator services.

Free MENA Alternatives to beIN Sports

  • SSC (Saudi Sports Channels): Free-to-air across Saudi Arabia + free streaming on the Saudi Sports app. Covers Saudi Pro League fully.
  • Shahid (basic tier): Free with ads, includes MBC sports clips and SSC content in some markets.
  • Arryadia & ON Sport: Free-to-air national broadcasters covering local leagues + AFCON.
  • Alkass: Free in Qatar; covers QSL and Qatari national team.
  • UEFA.tv: Free with signup. Carries some non-flagship UEFA matches in MENA.
  • Yalla Live: Free aggregator covering every Premier League, La Liga, Champions League, and World Cup match across MENA. No signup.

FAQs

Which channel shows Premier League in MENA? expand_more

beIN Sports holds exclusive Premier League rights across all 22 MENA countries. No free legal alternative for full matches in the region.

How much does a beIN Sports subscription cost? expand_more

Around AED 75-100/month in the UAE, QAR 100/month in Qatar, EGP 250-350/month in Egypt. Annual deals offer 25-33% discounts. The streaming-only beIN CONNECT runs slightly cheaper.

Can I watch football in Arabic commentary? expand_more

Yes — beIN Sports provides Arabic commentary on all 17 channels with English audio also selectable. SSC, Alkass, Arryadia, and ON Sport are all native Arabic. World Cup, AFCON, and Premier League are all commentated in Arabic across MENA.

What is TOD streaming? expand_more

TOD is beIN's standalone streaming service for MENA — cheaper than the full beIN Sports cable package (~AED 35-65/month). Mobile-first, no contract, includes most beIN Sports content but with restrictions on simultaneous device count.

How can I watch the Saudi Pro League free? expand_more

In Saudi Arabia, SSC channels are free-to-air. Outside Saudi, SSC streams via the Saudi Sports app (free with regional restrictions) or via Shahid VIP (~SAR 30/month).

Where can I watch AFCON in MENA? expand_more

AFCON is on beIN Sports across all MENA countries, plus free-to-air on the host nation's broadcaster and most North African countries' national TV channels. AFCON 2025 in Morocco was free on Arryadia.
