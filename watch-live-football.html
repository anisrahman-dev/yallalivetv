Football Competitions Available on Yalla Live

The platform isn't limited to one country or region — it's free for everyone, everywhere. Below are the major football competitions we cover live, with brief notes on dates, format, and where they're broadcast.

FIFA World Cup 2026

The 23rd edition of the FIFA Men's World Cup is co-hosted by the United States, Canada, and Mexico from June 11 to July 19, 2026. For the first time the tournament expands to 48 teams, divided into 12 groups of four, with the top two from each group plus the eight best third-placed teams advancing to a new round of 32. A total of 104 matches will be played across 16 host cities. Major broadcasters include FOX Sports and Telemundo (United States), ITV and the BBC (United Kingdom), beIN Sports (MENA), Sony Sports (India), and SBS (Australia).

UEFA Champions League

Europe's most prestigious club competition runs from September through May each season. The expanded "league phase" format introduced in 2024-25 continues — 36 clubs play eight matches each against different opponents, with the top eight advancing directly to the round of 16 and teams ranked 9-24 entering a knockout playoff. Main broadcasters: TNT Sports (UK), CBS Sports (US), beIN Sports (MENA), Sony Sports Network (India), and Movistar Plus+ (Spain).

UEFA Europa League

Europe's second-tier club competition uses the same 36-team league phase model as the Champions League. The winner earns automatic qualification to the following season's Champions League group stage. Main broadcasters: TNT Sports (UK), Paramount+ (US), beIN Sports (MENA), Sony Sports Network (India).

UEFA Conference League

The third-tier UEFA club competition, launched in 2021, gives smaller European clubs a chance to compete on a continental stage. Plays Thursdays alongside the Europa League. Main broadcasters: TNT Sports (UK), Paramount+ (US), beIN Sports (MENA).

English Premier League

England's top division features 20 clubs playing a double round-robin of 38 matchdays from August to May. The top four qualify for the Champions League, fifth-placed enters the Europa League, and the bottom three are relegated to the EFL Championship. Main broadcasters: Sky Sports and TNT Sports (UK), Peacock and USA Network (US), beIN Sports (MENA), Star Sports (India).

La Liga (Spain)

Spain's top flight, contested by 20 clubs across 38 rounds. The title race traditionally features Real Madrid and Barcelona, with Atlético Madrid, Athletic Bilbao, and Real Sociedad regularly in the European places. Main broadcasters: Movistar Plus+ and DAZN (Spain), ESPN+ (US), Premier Sports (UK), beIN Sports (MENA).

Serie A (Italy)

Italy's top division featuring 20 clubs across 38 matchdays. Champions earn Champions League qualification; teams placing 7th or 8th may enter the Conference League qualifying rounds. Headline clubs include Inter, Milan, Juventus, Napoli, and Roma. Main broadcasters: DAZN and Sky Sport (Italy), Paramount+ (US), TNT Sports (UK).

Bundesliga (Germany)

Germany's flagship competition with 18 clubs across 34 matchdays. Bayern Munich dominates historically, with Bayer Leverkusen, Borussia Dortmund, and RB Leipzig regularly challenging. The bottom two clubs are relegated directly; the 16th-placed side plays a relegation playoff. Main broadcasters: Sky Deutschland and DAZN (Germany), ESPN+ (US), Sky Sports (UK).

Ligue 1 (France)

France's top division features 18 clubs across 34 matchdays. Paris Saint-Germain has dominated for over a decade, while Marseille, Monaco, Lille, and Lyon regularly contest European places. Main broadcasters: Ligue 1+ and Amazon Prime Video (France), beIN Sports (MENA), Fubo Sports (US).

FA Cup

The world's oldest national football competition, dating back to 1871. Open to all eligible clubs from England's top 10 divisions, the FA Cup is famous for giant-killings and surprise upsets, with the final played at Wembley each May. Main broadcasters: BBC and ITV (UK), ESPN+ (US).

Africa Cup of Nations (AFCON)

CAF's flagship national-team competition, contested every two years by 24 nations. The 35th edition was hosted in Morocco from December 2025 to January 2026. The next edition is scheduled for Kenya, Tanzania, and Uganda in 2027. Main broadcasters: SuperSport (sub-Saharan Africa), beIN Sports (MENA), CAF TV (digital), and various national broadcasters across Africa.

Copa America

South America's premier national-team tournament, contested by CONMEBOL's 10 member associations plus invited guest nations. The most recent edition (2024, hosted in the United States) was won by Argentina; the next edition is scheduled for Ecuador in 2028. Main broadcasters: TyC Sports and DSports (South America), Fox Sports (US), Premier Sports (UK).

AFC Asian Cup

The continental championship of Asia, contested every four years. The next edition will be hosted by Saudi Arabia in 2027 and will expand to 24 nations. Qualifying matches and friendlies in the lead-up are indexed on Yalla Live throughout 2026 and 2027. Main broadcasters: Sony Sports (India), Astro (Malaysia), beIN Sports (MENA), Saudi Sports Channels (Saudi Arabia).

CONCACAF Gold Cup

The continental championship of North America, Central America, and the Caribbean. The 2025 edition was hosted by the United States from June to July 2025. The competition is held every two years and serves as a key warm-up for the 2026 World Cup co-hosts. Main broadcasters: FOX Sports and TUDN (US), TSN (Canada).

UEFA Nations League

A national-team competition contested by all 55 UEFA member associations every two years. Teams are split into four leagues (A, B, C, D) with promotion and relegation between them. The 2026-27 edition runs alongside the World Cup. Main broadcasters: UEFA.tv (free outside top markets), TNT Sports (UK), Fox Sports (US).

FIFA Club World Cup

The expanded 32-team Club World Cup launched in its new format in the United States from June 14 to July 13, 2025. The tournament now runs every four years and features club champions from all six confederations. Main broadcasters: DAZN (global, free), FOX Sports and Telemundo (US), beIN Sports (MENA).

Saudi Pro League

The top professional football league in Saudi Arabia, contested by 18 clubs. Globally watched since the 2023 wave of high-profile international signings reshaped the competition. Main broadcasters: SSC (Saudi Sports Channels) and Shahid VIP, with international rights on beIN Sports across MENA.

MLS — Major League Soccer

The top professional league in the United States and Canada. The 2026 regular season runs from late February through October, followed by the MLS Cup Playoffs. Main broadcaster: MLS Season Pass on Apple TV (global, exclusive), with select matches on Fox Sports and FS1 (US) and TSN (Canada).

Eredivisie (Netherlands)

The Dutch top flight features 18 clubs, with Ajax, PSV Eindhoven, and Feyenoord historically dominating the title race. Famous for developing world-class talent that goes on to bigger European leagues. Main broadcasters: ESPN (Netherlands), ESPN+ (US).

Primeira Liga (Portugal)

Portugal's top division featuring 18 clubs. The "Big Three" — Benfica, Porto, and Sporting CP — typically share the title race, with all three regularly featuring in European competition. Main broadcasters: Sport TV (Portugal), GolTV (US).
